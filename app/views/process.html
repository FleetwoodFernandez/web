• Process

    We believe in the power of a good idea, a firm wit, and plenty of research. Our process starts with a conceptual investigation of the questions posed by a new project, and is developed by the formal exploration of the many answers to those questions. We look to both abstractions and data in theory, history, and the arts to develop a guiding narrative that resolves the conflicting realities of program, site, and budget. We succeed when we find new ways of thinking about the dialogues and connections between people and place. We give equal rights to all tools of the trade, and we work in an open environment because collectively pursuing good ideas is more important to us than individual ownership over them.
